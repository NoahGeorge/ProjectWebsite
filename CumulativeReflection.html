Cumulative Reflection

Cumulative Reflection

Noah George

My three and a half years at Iowa State have been great. ISU football has been the best they have ever been with the best prospects they have ever had, the cross country team has had some of their best finishes in the NCAA Championship, but most importantly, I've learned a lot about engineering, and how to get it done right. I've taken classes that have given me a base to become a successful hardware engineer and how to work in or manage an engineering team. ISU has also provided me the opportunity to learn outside of the class in the form of undergraduate research with Prof. Peschel.

In my first year at Iowa State, I had the opportunity to work with Dr. Peschel and some other undergraduates on a computer vision research project. This research project aimed to use the OpenCV library to create a computer vision application that could identify and count the number of hogs that entered and exited a finishing facility. For this project, I had to learn how computer vision works and how we would apply it. One of the most interesting aspects of working on this project was being on the cutting edge of technology. It was enlightening seeing how powerful new technology could be. On the flip side, I learned how important it is to be up to date with current technology.

I've learned from my college experience that not knowing how to do something is no excuse for not doing it. If I don't know how to do something, the best path is to learn how to do it. A current example of this is my senior project. Our group is doing a research project about neural networks, something I don't have any experience with. This isn't an opportunity to give up, but rather a chance to learn something new that I might use in the future.

With respect to these things, I don't think there is much I would change about my undergraduate work. Obviously, it would be better if I had more experience, took more classes, and spent all of my free time becoming the perfect engineer. Still, I think I'm happy with how close I've gotten at ISU.
